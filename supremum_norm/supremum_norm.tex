\stitle{Supremum Norm}

\ssection{Why}

We want
a norm on
the vector space
of continuous functions.

\ssection{Definition}

Consider a function
from a closed real interval
to the real numbers.
The
\ct{absolute supremum}{absolutesupremum}
the supremum of the
absolute value of its results
on the interval.
Since the function is continuous
and defined on a closed interval,
the supremum is finite.


\ssubsection{Defining Result}

\begin{prop}
The functional mapping $f \in C[a, b]$
to its supremum is a norm.
\begin{proof}
Let $R$ denote the set of real numbers.
Define $\phi: C[a, b] \to R$ so that
\[
  \phi(f) = \sup \Set{\abs{f(x)}}{x \in [a, b]}.
\]
\begin{enumerate}
  \item
  $\abs{f(x)} \geq 0$ for all $x \in [a, b]$,
  so $\phi(f) \geq 0$.

  \item
  If $\phi(f) = 0$ then $\abs{f(x)} \leq 0$
    for all $x$ and so $f(x) = 0$ for all $x \in [a, b]$.
  If $f = 0$, then $\abs{f(x)} = 0$
  for all $x \in [a, b]$

  \item
  For all $\alpha$ real,
  $\abs{\alpha f(x)} = \abs{\alpha}\abs{f(x)}$.
  so $\phi(\alpha f) = \abs{\alpha}\phi(f)$

  \item
  For all $f, g \in C[a, b]$,
  and $x \in [a, b]$,
  $\abs{f(x) + g(x)} \leq \abs{f(x)} + \abs{g(x)}$
  by the triangle inequality for absolute value.
  Thus,
  \[
    \begin{aligned}
    \phi(f + g)
      &\leq \sup \Set{\abs{f(x)} + \abs{g(x)}}{x \in [a, b]} \\
      &\leq \sup \Set{\abs{f(x)}}{x \in [a,b]} + \sup \Set{\abs{g(x)}}{x \in [a, b]} \\
      &= \phi(f) + \phi(g)
    \end{aligned}
  \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{proof}
\end{prop}

As a result of this proposition,
we call the functional $\phi$
defined above the
\ct{supremum norm}{supremumnorm}.

\ssubsection{Notation}
Let $f \in C[a, b]$.
We denote the supremum norm of $f$
by $\supnorm{f}$.
