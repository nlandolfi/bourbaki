1. Why

If we have a norm, then we have a metric.

2. Motivating Result

Let $R$ be the set of real numbers.

\begin{prop} Let $(V, F)$ be a vector space. Let $f: V \to R$ be a norm. Let $g: V \times V \to R$ such that \[ g(x, y) = \norm{x - y}. \] Then $g$ is a metric. \begin{proof} \end{proof} \end{prop}
