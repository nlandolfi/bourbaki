1. Why

It is extremely common to use the first so many natural numbers as an index set.

2. Definition

A natural family of sets is a indexedkj family of sets whose index set is an subset of the natural numbers smaller than or equal to some specified natural number.

2.1 Notation

Let $n$ be a natural number. Let $A$ be a non-empty set. Let $a: \set{1, \dots, n} \to \powerset{A}$ We often denote the family of $a$ by $A_1, \dots, A_n$. We often say, \say{let $A_1, \dots, A_n$ be sets,} without any reference to the fact that we are thinking of a family.
