1. Why

We want to generalize operations beyond two objects.

2. Operations

The pairwise extension of a commutative operation is the function from finite families of the \rt{ground set}{groundset} to the \rt{ground set}{groundset} obtained by applying the operation pairwise to elements. TODO: this is not a function if the operation is not commutative.

The ordered pairwise extension of an operation is the function from finite families \rt{ground set}{groundset} to the \rt{ground set}{groundset} obtained by applying the operation pairwise to elements in order.

2.1 Notation

Let $(A, +)$ be an \rt{algebra}{algebra} and $\set{A_{i}}_{i=1}^{n}$ a finite family of elements of $A$. We denote the pairwise extension by \[ \overset{n}{\underset{i=1}{+}} A_i \]
