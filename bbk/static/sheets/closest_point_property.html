Suppose $H$ is a Hilbert space $A \subset H$ closed and convex. then if $x \in H$, there exists a unique $z \in A$ closest to $x$. There exists a unique $z \in A$ closest to $x$ such that \[ d(z, x) = \inf_{y \in A} d(y, x). \] \begin{proof} Take any sequence $\seq{y}$ such that \[ d(y_n, x) \to d = \inf_{y \in A} d(y, x) \] \end{proof}
