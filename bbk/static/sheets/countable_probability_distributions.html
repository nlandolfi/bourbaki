1. Why

We want to extend our notion of probability distribution to a set with infinite elements, but only countably many.

2. Why

Consider a set $A$. If $A$ has $n$ elements, then a probability distribution on $A$ is $p: A \to \R$ where $p(a) = 1/n$. There is a natural candidate.

What if $A$ is the set of natural numbers $\N$. The principle difficulty is that not all sequences of real numbers $a: \N \to \R$ are summable.

A (countable) probability distribution on $\N$ is $p: \N \to \R$ where $p \geq 0$ and \[ \sum_{n =1}^{\infty} p(n) = 1. \]
