2. Definition

A \t{subspace} of a vector space is a subset of vectors that is itself a vector space. In other words, a subspace is a subset of a vector space which is closed under vector addition and scalar multiplication.

3. Notation

Let $(V, \F)$ be a vector space. Let $U \subset V$ with $$ \alpha u + \beta v \in U $$ for all $\alpha, \beta \in \F$ and $u, v \in U$. Then $U$ is a subspace of $(V, \F)$.

4. Examples

The entire set of vectors is a subspace. The set consisting only of the zero vector is a subspace; we call this the \t{zero subspace}. These two subspaces are called \t{trival subspaces}. A \t{nontrivial subspace} is a subspace that is not trivial.

5. Properties

\begin{prop} The intersection of a family of subspaces is a subspace. \end{prop}

\begin{prop} There exists a family of subspaces whose union is not a subspace;

\begin{remark} In other words: the union of a family subspaces need not be a subspace. \end{remark}

\begin{prop} A subspace must contain the zero vector; in other words, every subspace is nonempty. \end{prop}
