2. Definition

Let $S$ be the sphere in $\R^3$ and let $N = (0, 0, 1)$, the north pole. Then the \t{stereopgraphic projection} of $S - \set{N}$ is the function $\pi: S - \set{N} \to \R^2$ defined as follows. The point $\pi(p)$ is the $(x, y)$ values for the point where the line through $N$ and $p$ intersects the plane plane $P = \set{(x, y, z) \in \R^3}{z = 0}$ (the "xy-plane}).\footnote{Future editions will expand."

\begin{proposition} The stereographic projection is a diffeomorphism. \end{proposition}
