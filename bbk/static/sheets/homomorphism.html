1. Why

We name a \rt{function}{function} which preserves \rt{group}{group} structure.

2. Definition

A homomorphism from \rt{group}{group} $(A, +)$ to \rt{group}{group} $(B, \tilde{+})$ is a function $f: A \to B$ such that $f(e_A) = f(e_B)$ for \rt{identities}{identityelement} $e_A \in A$ and $e_B \in B$ and $f(a + a') = f(a) \tilde{+} f(a')$ for all $a, a' \in A$.
