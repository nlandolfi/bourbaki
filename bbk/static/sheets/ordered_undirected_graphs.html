1. Why

Need for perfect elimination orderings.

2. definition

An \t{ordering} of an undirected graph is a numbering of its vertices. An \t{ordered undirected graph} is a triple: the first two objects as an ordered pair are an undirected graph and the third object is a numbering of this graphs vertices.

3. Notation

Let $G = (V, E)$ be an undirected graph with $n = \card{V}$. Let $\sigma: \set{1, 2, \dots, n} \to V$ be a numbering of $V$. We denote the ordered graph consisting of $G$ and $\sigma$ as $G_{\sigma} = (V, E, \sigma)$.
