1. Why

Can flashlights be made to speak?

Speech can be made to correspond to words. And words are sequences of letters. So, roughly, can we communicate letters with a flashlight? The trick is: no sounds.

2. Discussion

Well, what can we do with a flashlight? Turn it on and off. So we can show blinks of light. Here's a thought: One blink means $a$. Two blinks means $b$. Three blinks means $c$. Four blinks means $d$. And so on.

Here's the rub: how do we know when one letter stops, and another begins. Put another way, for communicating with flashlights the word $(b, a, d)$ should we blink the flashlight $2 + 1 + 4 = 7$ times. Or is that the letter corresponding to seven blinks, namely the letter $g$. If we could change the color of the flashlight, we could use this to signal a change in letters. Or if we could make noise, we could beep Each time the color changes its a new letter.

Here's a simple counter: wait for a while in between letters. And wait for even longer in between words.
