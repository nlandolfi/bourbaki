1. Why

We want to solve equations with squares.

2. Definition

Let $b$ be any real number. We want to find $a$ to solve $$ a^2 = b. $$ We call a solution $a$ a \t{square root} of $b$.

\begin{prop} A real number has two square roots. \end{prop}

Despite the above proposition, we still speak of \t{the square root} of a real number, which is the positive square root. We also speak of the \t{square root function} which associates a real number to its positive square root.

3. Why

As with natural numbers, we denote the square root of $x$ by $\sqrt{x}$.
