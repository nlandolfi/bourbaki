2. Definition

Let $Z$ and $X$ be sets, either of which may or may not be finite.

A \t{generative conditional distribution} (or \t{latent variable model}) with discrete (if $X$ finite, continuous otherwise) \t{observations} $X$ and discrete (if $Z$ finite, continuous otherwise) \t{latents} $Z$ is an ordered pair $(\set{p_z}, \set{p_{x \mid z}})$ whose first coordinate is a distribution (density) family on $Z$ and whose second coordinate is a conditional distribution (density) family on $X$ from $Z$.

The \t{joint function} $p_{zx}: Z \times X \to \R$ is defined by $p_{zx}(\zeta, \xi) = p_{z}(\zeta)p_{x \mid z}(\xi, \zeta)$ for all $\xi \in X$ and $\zeta \in Z$. It is may be distribution (density) if (but not only if), for example both $p_{z}$ and $p_{x \mid z}$ are distributions (densities).

A \t{generative family} is a family of latent distributions $\set{(\set{p^{(\theta)}_z}, \set{p^{(\theta)}_{x \mid z}})}_{\theta \in \Theta}$. In this case we denote the joint function $p_{zx}^{(\theta)}$ to indicate the dependence on the \t{parameters} $\theta$.

A \t{deep conditional family} is a neural network parameterized conditional distribution family. A \t{deep generative family} (or \t{deep latent variable model}, \t{DLVM}) is a neural network parameterized generative conditional family.
