2. Definition

A matrix $A$ is \t{positive definite} if all its quadratic forms are positive.

2.1 Notation

Let $A \in \RMat{n}{n}$. $A$ is positive definite if for every $x \in \RVec{d}$, $$ x^T A x > 0. $$ We denote the set of real-valued positive definite $d$ by $d$ matrices by $\SymPDMat{d}$.
