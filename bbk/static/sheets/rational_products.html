1. Why

We want to multiply rationals.\footnote{Future editions will expand on this why.}

2. Definition

Let $\eqc{(a, b)}, \eqc{(b, c)} \in \Q$. The \t{rational product} of $\eqc{(a, b)}$ with $\eqc{(b, c)}$ is $\eqc{(ac , bd)}$.\footnote{An account that this is well-defined will appear in future editions.}

2.1 Notation

We denote the rational product of $q, r \in \Q$ by $q \cdot r$.
