1. Why

What is the multiplicative inverse of $\eqc{(a, b)}$ in the rationals?

2. Result

\begin{proposition} The multiplicative inverse of $\eqc{(a, b)} \in \Q$ if $b \neq 0_{\Z}$ is $\eqc{(b,a)}$. \end{proposition}

2.1 Notation

We denote the multiplicative inverse of $q \in \Q$ by $\inv{q}$. We denote $q \cdot (\inv{r})$ by $q/r$.

2.2 Division

We call the operation $(a, b) \mapsto a/b$ \t{rational division}.
