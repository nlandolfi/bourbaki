\stitle{Central Limit Theorem}

1. Why

The sum of several independent and identically distributed random variables tends toward a normal distribution. TODO, and defined "tends"

2. Result

\begin{prop} Let $(X, \SA, \mu)$ be a probability space. Let $\seq{f}$ be a sequence of independent and identically distributed real-valued random variables on $X$ with $\Expect(\seqt{f}) = \mu < \infty$ and $\var(\seqt{f}) = \sigma^2 < \infty$ for all $n$. Define $s_n = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}f_i$. For all real numbers $t$, \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \mu\parens*{\Set*{ x \in X }{ \frac{ s_n(x) - n\mu }{ \sigma\sqrt{n} } \leq t }} = \Phi(t). \] \end{prop}
