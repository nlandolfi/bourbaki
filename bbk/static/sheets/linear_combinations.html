2. Definition

A \t{linear combination} is a sequence of vectors and a sequence of scalars from a vector space. The \t{result of a linear combination} is the result of scaling the $i$th vector by the $i$th scalar and adding the results.

A \t{trivial linear combination} is one whose sequence of scalars is zero at each coordinate. A \t{nontrivial linear combination} is one which is not trivial: in other words, there exists on coordinate of its scalar sequence which is nonzero.

2.1 Notation

Let $(V, \F)$ be a vector space. Let $v = (v_1, \dots, v_n)$ be a sequence of vectors in $V$. Let $a = (a_1, \dots, a_n)$ be a sequence of scalars in $\F$. Then $(v, a)$ is a linear combination and we can express its result by $$ a_1v_1 + a_2v_2 + \cdots + a_n v_n. $$
