1. Why

We are constantly thinking of the integers as the endpoints of equal length segments of a line.

2. Discussion

We commonly associate elements of the integers with the endpoints of equal-length segments of a real line. Take segment $S_0$ of $L$ with endpoints $p$ and $q$. Associate the point $p$ with $0$. Associate the point $q$ with $1$. Take a segment $S_1$ of equal length, non-overlapping with $S_0$, who shares the endpoint $q$. Associate the second endpoint of this segment $2$. Continue with the rest.\footnote{Future editions will expand.}

3. Distance

We can define a function $f: \Z \to \Z$ so that $f(a, b) = a - b$ if $a > b$ and $b - a$ if $b > a$. Notice that $f$ is symmetric: $f(a,b) = f(b, a)$. We interpret $f$ as the \t{distance} between two integers $a, b \in \Z$ if we associate the integers with integral segments. If $a > b$, then the distance between $a$ and $b$ is $a - b$.
