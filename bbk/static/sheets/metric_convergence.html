1. Why

Once we have a notion of distance, we can define a more general notion of convergence.

2. Definition

A sequence of elements of a metric space converges to an fixed element of the space if the sequence of distances between the elements of the fixed element converges to zero.

2.1 Notation

Let $(A, d)$ be a metric space. Let $\seq{a}$ be a sequence in $A$ Then $\set{a}$ converges to $a_0$ if $d(a_n, a) \to 0$.
