1. Why

We want to talk about making a sequence of decisions.

2. Definition

Let $S$ and $A$ be finite sets. Let $T: S \times A \to (S \to [0, 1])$ so that for each $s \in S$ and $a \in A$, $T_{sa}: S \to [0, 1]$ is a probability distribution over $S$. We call the ordered triple $(S, A, T)$ a \t{finite state-action process}.

A \t{trajectory} in the \t{state set} $S$ and \t{action set} $A$ is a sequence in $S \times A$.

Let $r: S \times A \times S \to \R $, $N \in \N $.
