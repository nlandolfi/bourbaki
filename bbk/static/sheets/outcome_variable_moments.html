\section*{Definition}

Let $x: \Omega \to \R $ a random variable and $p: \Omega \to \R $ a distribution. The \t{mean square} of $x$ is $\E (x^2)$. Define $\mu = \E (x)$. We can express $\E (x^2) = \E (x)^2 + \cov(x)$ since \[ \begin{aligned} \cov(x) = \E ((x -\mu )^2) &= \E (x^2 - 2\mu x + \mu ^2) \\ &= \E (x^2) -2\mu \E (x) + \mu ^2 \\ &= \E (x^2) - \mu ^2. \end{aligned} \] We refer to this relation as the \t{mean-variance decomposition} of $x$. \ifhmode\unskip\fi\footnote{ Future editions will modify this sheet, and likely motivate this decomposition in terms of minimum square error estimation. }

The \t{n'th moment} of $x$ is $\E (x^n)$. The mean is the first moment. The covariance is the second moment minus the square of the first moment.
