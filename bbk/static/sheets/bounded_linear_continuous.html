1. Why

All continuous functions between norm spaces are bounded linear functions.

2. Result

\begin{prop} Let $((V_1, F), \norm{\cdot}_1)$ and Let $((V_2, F), \norm{\cdot}_2)$ be two norm spaces. Let $f: V_1 \to V_2$ be a linear function between two norm spaces. Then \begin{enumerate} \item $\exists x \in V_1$ such that $f$ is continuous at $x$,

\item $f$ is continuous,

\item $f$ is uniformly continuous, and

\item $f$ is bounded, \end{enumerate} are all equivalent conditions. \end{prop}
