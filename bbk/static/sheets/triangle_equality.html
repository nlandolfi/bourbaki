1. Why

The squared norm of a sum of orthogonal vectors is the sum of their squared norms.

2. Result

\begin{prop} Let $(V, F)$ be an inner product space with induced norm $\norm{\cdot}$. Let $x, y \in V$ be orthgonal vector. Then \[ \norm{x + y}^2 = \norm{x}^2 + \norm{y}^2. \] \end{prop}
