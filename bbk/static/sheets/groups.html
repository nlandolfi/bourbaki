1. Why

We generalize the algebraic structure of addition over the integers.

2. Definition

A group is an \rt{algebra}{algebra} with: (1) an associative operation, (2) an identity element, and (3) an inverse for each element. We call the operation of the algebra group addition. A commutative group is a group whose operation commutes.
