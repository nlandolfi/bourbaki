1. Why

We generalize the algebraic structure of addition over the integers.

2. Definition

A \t{group} is an \t{algebra} with: (1) an associative operation, (2) an identity element, and (3) an inverse for each element. We call the operation of the algebra \t{group addition}. A \t{commutative group} is a group whose operation commutes. A commutative group is also sometimes called an \t{Abelian group}.
