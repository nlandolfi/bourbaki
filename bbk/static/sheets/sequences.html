1. Why

We want to speak of infinite processes, and to do so we define sequences indexed by $\N$. In other words, important families are those indexed by the natural numbers.

2. Definition

A \t{sequence} (\t{infinite sequence}) is a family whose index set is $\N$ (the set of natural numbers without zero). The \t{$n$th term} or \t{coordinate} of a sequence is the result of the $n$th natural number, $n \in \N$. \ifhmode\unskip\fi\footnote{ Future editions may also comment that we are introducing language for the steps of an infinite process. }

2.1 Notation

Let $A$ be a non-empty set and $a: \N \to A$. Then $a$ is a (infinite) sequence in $A$. $a(n)$ is the $n$th term. We also denote $a$ by $\seq{a}$ and $a(n)$ by $\seqt{a}$. If $\set{A_n}_{n \in \N}$ is an infinite sequence of sets, then we denote the direct product of the sequence by $\prod_{i = 1}^{\infty} A_i$.

3. Natural unions and intersections

We denote the family of the infinite sequence of sets $\seq{A}$ by $\union_{i = 1}^{\infty} A_i$. Similarly, we denote the intersection of an infinite sequence of sets by $\intersection_{i = 1}^{\infty} A_i$, respectively.
