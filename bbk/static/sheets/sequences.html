1. Why

We introduce language for the steps of an infinite process.

2. Definition

A \t{finite sequence} is a family whose index set is a natural number (excluding zero). Some authors call a finite sequence, in certain contexts, a \t{numbering} or, in other contexts, a \t{string}. An \t{infinite sequence} is a family whose index set is the set of natural numbers (without zero). The \t{$n$th term} of a sequence (finite or infinite) is the result of the $n$th natural number. Let $A$ be a non-empty set. A sequence \t{in} $A$ is a function from the natural numbers to the set.

2.1 Notation

Let $A$ be a non-empty set. Let $a: \N \to A$ Then $a$ is a sequence in $A$. $a(n)$ is the $n$th term. We also denote $a$ by $\seq{a}$ and $a(n)$ by $\seqt{a}$.

3. Natural Unions and intersections

If $\set{A_i}$ is a finite sequence of sets indexed by $\upto{n}$, then we denote the union of the family by \[ \union_{i = 1}^{n} A_i \] If $\set{A_i}$ is an infinite sequence of sets, then we denote the union of the family by \[ \union_{i = 1}^{\infty} A_i. \] Similarly, we denote the intersections of a finite and infinite sequence of sets $\set{A_i}$ by \[ \intersection_{i = 1}^{n} A_i \quad \text{ and } \quad \intersection_{i = 1}^{\infty} A_i. \] respectively.
