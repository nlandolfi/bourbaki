\section*{Definition}

Let $A \in \R ^{l \times m}$ and $B \in \R ^{m \times n}$. In this case we call $A$ and $B$ \t{conformable}. The \t{matrix-matrix product} of $A$ and $B$ is the matrix $C \in \R ^{l \times n}$ whose $i$th row $c_i$ (for $i = 1, \dots , n$) is defind $c_i = Ab_i$ where $b_i$ is the $i$th row of $B$.

\subsection*{Notation}

We denote the matrix product of $A$ and $B$ by $AB$.

\subsection*{Properties}

Future editions will contain accounts of the following basic properties.

\begin{proposition} Matrix multiplication is associative. \end{proposition}

\begin{proposition} Matrix multiplication is not commutative. \end{proposition} Indeed, the matrix-matrix produce of $B$ and $A$ may not even be defined, if $B$ and $A$ are not conformable.
