1. Why

A natural distribution to associate with a dataset is to assign probabilities which are the proporitions.

2. Definition

The empirical distribution of a dataset is to assign probabilities to outcomes in accordance to their proportions of appearance in the dataset. The proporitions are nonnegative and sum to one, so this definition yields in a probability distribution.

2.1 Notation

Let $(a^1, \dots, a^n)$ be a data set in $A$. Let $q: A \to \R$ be defined by \[ q(a) = \frac{1}{n} \card{\Set*{k \in \set{1, \dots, n}}{a^k = a}} \] Then $q$ is the empirical distribution of $(a^1, \dots, a^n)$.
