1. Why

Suppose we are going to make a bunch of measurements. We associate to each element of a finite set a number. If the measurements we make are divided into proporitions according to a distribution, if we make all the measurements and then average the results what do we expect to get.

2. Definition

Consider a distribution $p$ and a real-valued function $f$. The \ct{distribution expectation} of $f$ under $p$ is the sum of the product of the results of $p$ and $f$ on the elements of the set.

2.1 Notation

Let $A$ a finite set. Let $R$ denote the set of real numbers. Let $p: A \to R$. Let $f: A \to R$. The expectation of $f$ under $p$ is \[ \sum_{a \in A} p(a) f(a). \]
