2. Definition

The law of a real-valued random variable, using the topological sigma algebra, is the image measure of the probability measure under the random variable Recall that

2.1 Notation

Let $(X, \mathcal{A})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{B})$ be two measurable spaces. Let $f: X \to Y$ be a random variable. Let $\mu: \mathcal{A} \to \nneri$ be a probability measure. We denote the law of $f$ by $\rvlaw{\mu}{f}$.
