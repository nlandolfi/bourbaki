\section*{Definition}

The \t{expectation} (or \t{expected value}) of a real-valued random variable defined on a probability space is its integral with respect to the probability measure. The expectation of a random variable is also called its \t{mean}.

0.1 Notation

Let $(X, \mathcal{A} , \mu )$ be a probability space and $f: X \to \R $ be a random variable. We denote the expectation of $f$ by $\E f$, defined: \[ \E f = \int f d\mu . \]

Expectation

Definition

The expectation (or expected value) of a real-valued random variable defined on a probability space is its integral with respect to the probability measure. The expectation of a random variable is also called its mean.

\ssubsection{Notation}

Let $(X, 𝒜, μ)$ be a probability space and $f: X → 𝗥$ be a random variable. We denote the expectation of $f$ by $𝗘f$, defined:

\[ \E f = \int f d\mu . \]
