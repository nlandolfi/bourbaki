2. Definition

The \t{matrix-vector product} between an $m \times n$-matrix and an $n$-vector is the result of the linear combination of the columns of the matrix with the sequence of scalars in the vector. So the matrix-vector product is an $m$-vector.

2.1 Notation

Let $C$ be a nonempty set. Let $A \in \Mat{C}{m}{n}$ and let $x \in \Vec{C}{n}$. As usual, we denote the matrix-vector product of $A$ with $x$ by $Ax$, read "A x," and defined by \[ b_i = \sum_{j = 1}^{n} a_{ij}x_j \] for $i \in \upto{m}$. Let \[ A = \bmat{ & a_1^\tp & \\ & a_2^\tp & \\ & \vdots & \\ & a_{m}^\tp & } \] Or, if $a_i^\tp$ is the $i$th row of $A$, then \[ b_i = a_i^\tp x \] for $i \in \upto{m}$.

\ssection{Definition}

The matrix-vector product between an $m × n$-matrix and an $n$-vector is the result of the linear combination of the columns of the matrix with the sequence of scalars in the vector. So the matrix-vector product is an $m$-vector.

\ssubsection{Notation}

Let $C$ be a nonempty set. Let $A ∈ \Mat{C}{m}{n}$ and let $x ∈ \Vec{C}{n}$. As usual, we denote the matrix-vector product of $A$ with $x$ by $Ax$, read “A x,” and defined by

\[ b_i = ∑_{j = 1}^{n} a_{ij}x_j \]

for $i ∈ \upto{m}$. Let

\[ A = \bmat{ ❲&❳ a_1^\tp ❲&❳
❲&❳ a_2^\tp ❲&❳
❲&❳ ⋮ ❲&❳
❲&❳ a_{m}^\tp ❲&❳ } \]

 Or, if $a_i^\tp$ is the $i$th row of $A$, then

\[ b_i = a_i^\tp x \]

for $i ∈ \upto{m}$.
