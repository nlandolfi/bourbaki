2. Definition

The \t{matrix-vector product} between an $m \times n$-matrix and an $n$-vector is the result of the linear combination of the columns of the matrix with the sequence of scalars in the vector. So the matrix-vector product is an $m$-vector.

2.1 Notation

Let $C$ be a nonempty set. Let $A \in \Mat{C}{m}{n}$ and let $x \in \Vec{C}{n}$. We denote the matrix-vector product of $A$ with $x$ by $Ax$, read "A x".

If we denote $Ax$ by $b$, then $$ b_i = \sum_{j = 1}^{n} a_{ij}x_j $$ for $i \in \upto{m}$. Let $$ A = \bmat{ &a_1^T& \\ &a_2^T& \\ &\vdots & \\ & a_{m}^T& } $$ Or, if $a_i^T$ is the $i$th row of $A$, then $$ b_i = a_i^T x $$ for $i \in \upto{m}$.
