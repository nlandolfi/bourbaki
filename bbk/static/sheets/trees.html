1. Why

Tree branches split and do not recombine. We formalize this property in the language of graphs.

2. Definition

A tree is a connected acyclic graph.

2.1 Notation

We denote trees by $T$, a mnemonic for "tree." Let $u, v$ be two vertices connected in $T$. We denote that the edge between $u$ and $v$ exists by writing $\set{u, v} \in T$.

3. Properties

\begin{prop} There is only one path between any two vertices in a tree.

\begin{proof} Suppose to the contrary that there were two paths from vertex $u$ to vertex $v$, then by combining these paths we obtain a cycle. But the tree has no cycles, so there must not be two paths between any two vertices. \end{proof} \end{prop}
