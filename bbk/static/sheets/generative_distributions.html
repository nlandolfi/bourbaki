2. Definition

Let $Z$ and $X$ be sets, either of which may or may not be finite.

A \t{latent generation pair} for \t{observations} $X$ from \t{latents} $Z$ is an ordered pair $(p_z, p_{x \mid z})$ whose first coordinate is a distribution (density) on $Z$ and whose second coordinate is a conditional distribution (density) on $X$ from $Z$.

The \t{joint function} $p_{zx}: Z \times X \to \R$ of the pair is defined by $p_{zx}(\zeta, \xi) = p_{z}(\zeta)p_{x \mid z}(\xi, \zeta)$ for all $\xi \in X$ and $\zeta \in Z$. It is a distribution (density) if (not only if) both $p_{z}$ and $p_{x \mid z}$ are distributions (densities). Regardless, we define the \t{marginal function} $p_x: X \to \R$ by $p_x(\xi) = \int_Z p_{zx}(\xi, \cdot)$. It too may be a distribution, density, or neither. In cases we construct, it is often one a distribution or a density, but it need not be either.

2.1 Interpretation as distribution graph

Clearly, a latent generation pair on $Z$ and $X$ is isomorphic to a graph distribution on nodes $\set{1, 2}$ with a directed edge $(1, 2)$, domains $Z$ and $X$ and distributions which are the first and second coordinate of the pair.

3. Parametrizations

By parameterizing either or both of the coordinates of the pair, we have a \t{generative distribution family}, or, when there is no possibility of ambiguity, a \t{generative family}. A \t{deep generative family} is one whose parameterizer is a neural network.

3.1 Other terminology

Other terminology for latent generation pair includes \t{latent variable model}. Some authorities refer to the marginal function as the \t{generative model}, still others use this term to refer to the pair.
