2. Definition

Two events are independent if the probability of their intersection is the product of their respective probabilities.

3. Definition

Let $\PE$ be an event probability function with set of outcomes $A$. Then the events $B$ and $C$ are independent if \[ \PE(B \intersect C) = \PE(B)\PE(C). \]
