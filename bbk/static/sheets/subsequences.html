1. Why

We want to select particular terms of sequence.

2. Definition

A \t{subindex} is a monotonically increasing function from and to the natural numbers. Roughly, it selects some ordered infinite subset of natural numbers. A \t{subsequence} of a first sequence is any second sequence which is the composition of the first sequence with a subindex.

3. Notation

Let $i: \N \to \N $ such that $n < m \Rightarrow i(n) < i(m)$. Then $i$ is a subindex. Let $b = a \comp i$. Then $b$ is a subsequence of $a$. We denote it by $\set{b_{i(n)}}_n$ and the $n$th term by $b_{i(n)}$.
