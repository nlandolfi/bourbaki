1. Why

We bound the probability that a random variance deviates from its mean using its variance.

2. Result

\begin{prop} Let $f$ be a square-integrable real-valued random variable on the probability space $(X, \SA, \mu)$. Then for $t > 0$, \[ \mu\parens*{\abs{f - \Expect(f)} \geq t} \leq \frac{\var f}{t^2}. \] \begin{proof} The set $\abs{f - \Expect(f)} \geq t$ is $\Set*{x \in X}{ \abs{f(x) - \Expect(f)} \geq t}$. This set is $\Set*{x \in X}{({f(x) - \Expect(f)})^2 \geq t^2}$. By using the non-negative inequality \[ \mu(\Set*{x \in X}{({f(x) - \Expect(f)})^2 \geq t^2}) \leq \frac{\Expect(f - \Expect(f))}{t^2}. \] We recognize the numerator of the right hand side as the variance. \end{proof} \end{prop}

The above is also called Chebychev's Inequality
