2. Definition

Consider two events, the second of which has non-zero probability. The conditional probability of the first event conditioned on a second event is the result of dividing the probability of the second event into the probability of the intersection of the two events.

2.1 Notation

Let $\PE$ be the event probability function. Let $A$ and $B$ be two events with $\PE(B) \neq 0$. Then the conditional probability of $A$ conditioned on $B$ is \[ \frac{\PE(A \intersect B)}{\PE(B)}. \] We denote this by $\PE(A \mid B)$.
