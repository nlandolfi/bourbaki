\section{Definition}

Let $A \subset \R$ open. Let $f: A \to \R$ be differentiable with derivative $f': \R \to \R$. We call $f$ \t{twice differentiable} (or \t{two times differentiable}) if its derivative $f'$ is differentiable. In this case, we call the derivative of $f'$ the \t{second derivative} of $f$.

1.1 Notation

Let $A \subset R$. The second derivative of the twice-differentiable function $f: A \to \R$ is sometimes denoted $f''(x): A \to \R$
