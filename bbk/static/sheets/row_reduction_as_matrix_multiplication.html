1. Why

The matrix of a linear system and its row reduction are related by a matrix multiplication.

2. Main observation

\begin{proposition} Let $(A \in \R^{n \times n}, b \in \R^{n})$ be a linear system. Let $(C, d)$ be the $ij$-reduction of $(A, b)$. There exists $L \in \R^{n \times n}$ so that \[ C = LA \text{ and } d = Lb. \] \begin{proof} Define $L \in \R^{n \times n}$ by $L_{ii} = 0$ and \[ L_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{ if } i = j \\ -A_{kj}/A_{ij} & \text{ if } k \neq i \text{ and } A_{kj} \neq 0 \\ 0 & \text{ otherwise }. \end{cases} \] \end{proof} \end{proposition}

A simple consequence is that there exists $(L_1, \dots, L_{n-1})$ in $\R^{n \times n}$ of lower triangular matrices so that the ordinary row reduction of $(A, b)$ is $(L_{n-1}\cdots L_2 L_1 A, L_{n-1}\cdots L_2 L_1b)$.

3. For example

\[ L = \bmat{ 1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ -A_{21}/A_{11} & 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ -A_{31}/A_{11} & 0 & 1 & \ddots & 0 \\ \vdots & 0 & \ddots & \ddots & 0 \\ -A_{n1}/A_{11} & 0 & 1 & \ddots & 0 \\ } \]
