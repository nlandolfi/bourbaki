1. Why

We provide a principle for distribution selection based on maximizing the probability of the dataset.

2. Definition

We have a set of outcomes $A$ and a dataset $(a^1, \dots, a^n)$. We want a distribution $p: A \to \R$. We define $\bar{p}: A^n \to \R$ by $\bar{p}(a^1, \dots, a^n) = \prod_{i = 1}^{n} p(a^i)$.

The principle of maximum likelihood says to solve: \[ \maximization{p, \text{ a distribution }}{\bar{p}(a^1, \dots, a^n)} \]
