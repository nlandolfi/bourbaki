1. Why

We often take the topology of a topological space as the generating set for the sigma algebra.

2. Definition

Given a topological space, the \t{topological sigma algebra} is the sigma algebra generated by the topology.

3. Notation

Let $(A, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space. We denote the topological sigma algebra by $\sigma(\mathcal{T})$.
