1. Why

Given $n$ sets each with metrics, there is a standard way of turning the direct product of the sets into a metric space. In other words, defining a distance on the tuples of elements from the sets.

2. Motivating Result

\begin{prop} Let $(A_1, d_1), \dots, (A_n, d_n)$ be metric spaces. Let $A$ be $\prod_{i = 1}^n A_n$ and let $R$ be the set of real numbers. Define $d: A \times A \to R$ by \[ d(a, b) = \max\set{d_1(a_1, b_1), \dots, d_n(a_n, b_n)}. \] Then $(A, d)$ is a metric space. \end{prop}

We call $d$ the \t{product metric}.
