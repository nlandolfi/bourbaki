1. Why

We want to talk about probability on the set of outcomes which is the real numbers.

2. Definition

A \t{probability density} or \t{probability density function} is a real-valued function on the reals which is nonnegative and normalized. A real-valued function on the reals is \t{normalized} if it integrates to 1.

3. Notation

Let $f: \R \to \R$ with $f \geq 0$ and $\int f = 1$. Then $f$ is a probability density.
