\ssection{Definition}

Let $a, b \in \Z$.
$a$ is a \t{divisor} of $b$ if there exists $k > 0$ so that $ak = b$.

If instead $b = ak + r$ where $r > 0$ and $r < a$, then we call $r$ the \t{remainder} of dividing $a$ into $b$.

\ssection{Notation}

We denote the remainder of dividing $a$ into $b$ by $b \mod a$.
