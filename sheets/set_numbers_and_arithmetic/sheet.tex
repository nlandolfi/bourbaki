\section*{Why}

How does the number of elements change with unions, and products.

\section*{Results}

There are a few nice relations.\footnote{Proofs will appear in future editions.}
Recall from \sheetref{finite_sets}{Finite Sets}that the union and product of finite sets is finite.
Also, the power of a finite set is finite.

\begin{proposition}
Let $A$ and $B$ be finite sets with $A \cap  B = \varnothing$.
Then $\num{A \cup B} = \num{A} + \num{B}$.\end{proposition}
\begin{proposition}
Let $A$ and $B$ be a finite sets
Then $\num{A \times  B} = \num{A} \cdot  \num{B}$.\end{proposition}
\begin{proposition}
Let $A$ and $B$ be a finite sets
Then $\num{A^B} = \num{A}^{\num{B}}$.\end{proposition}
\begin{proposition}
Let $A$ be a finite set.
Then $\num{\powerset{A}} = 2^{\nu m{A}}$.\end{proposition}
