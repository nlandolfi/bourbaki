
\section*{Why}

The density of a random
variable determines
the cumulative distribution
function.

\section*{Result}

\begin{proposition}
A probability density
function of a random variable,
if it exists,
characterizes the cumulative
distribution function.
\begin{proof}Let $(X, \mathcal{A} , \mu )$ be a
probability space.
Let $f$ be a real-valued
random variable on $X$.
Let $\lambda $ denote
the cover length.
Let $g$ be a probability
density of $f$:
Then:
\[
\begin{aligned}
\rvcdf{f}(t)
&= \mu (\Set*{x \in X}{f(x) \in (-\infty, t])}) \\
&= \int_{(-\infty, t]} gd\lambda .
\end{aligned}
\]\end{proof}
\end{proposition}
