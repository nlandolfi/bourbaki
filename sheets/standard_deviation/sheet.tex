\section*{Definition}

The \t{standard deviation} of a real-valued random variable is the square root of its variance.

\subsection*{Notation}

Let $f$ be an integrable real-valued random variable.
Denote the standard deviation of $f$ by $\std(f)$.
We have defined it
  \[
\std(f) = \sqrt{\var(f)}.
  \]
