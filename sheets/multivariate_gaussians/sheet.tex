\ssection{Why}

We generalize the Gaussian
to $n$-dimensional space.

\ssection{Definition}

$f: \R^d \to \R$ is a \ct{gaussian density}{}
if there exists $\Sigma \succ 0$
and $\mu$ such that
\[
  f(x) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^d \det \Sigma}
  \exp(-\frac{1}{2} (x - \mu)^T \Sigma^{-1} (x - \mu)
\]
