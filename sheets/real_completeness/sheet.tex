\ssection{Why}

Is the set of real numbers a complete ordered field (in the sense of \sheetref{complete_fields}{Complete Fields}?

\ssection{Main Result}

\begin{proposition}
  $(\R, +, \cdot, <)$ is a complete ordered field.\footnote{The account will appear in future editions.}
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
  The supremum of a set of nonempty real numbers bounded from above $R$ is $\union R$.
\end{proof}
