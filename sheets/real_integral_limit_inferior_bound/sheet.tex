\begin{prop}

The integral
of the limit inferior
of a sequence
of measurable, nonnegative,
extended-real-valued
functions is
no larger than
the limit inferior
of the sequence
of integrals.

\begin{proof}

Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$
be a measure space,
and let $\seqt{f}: \to \nneri$
a $\mathcal{A}$-measurable function
for every natural number $n$.
We want to show that if
\[
  \int \liminf_n f_n d\mu \leq \liminf_n \int f_n d\mu.
\]

\end{proof}

\end{prop}
