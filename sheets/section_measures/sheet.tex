\ssection{Why}

Toward a theory
of iterated integrals,
we need to know the function
measuring a section
is integrable.

\ssection{Results}

\begin{prop}
Let $(X, \SA, \mu)$
and $(Y, \SB, \nu)$
be sigma-finite
measurable spaces.
Let $E \in \SA \cross \SB$.
The function
  $x \mapsto \nu(E_x)$
  is $\SA$-measurable
  and the function
  $y \mapsto \mu(E^y)$
  is $\SB$-measurable.
