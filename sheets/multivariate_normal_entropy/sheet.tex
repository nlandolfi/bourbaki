
\section*{Result}

\begin{proposition}
Let $x: \Omega  \to \R ^d$ be a normally distributed random variable on a probability space $(\Omega , \mathcal{A} , \mathbfsf{P} )$ with mean $\mu  \in \R ^d$ and covariance $\Sigma \succ 0$. Let $g: \R ^d \to \R $ be the density of $x$. Then the entropy of $x$ is
\[
h(g) = -\int g \log g = \frac{1}{2} \log ((2\pi  e)^d \det\Sigma )
\]
\end{proposition}

This result tells us the \t{multivariate normal entropy} or \t{Gaussian entropy}.
