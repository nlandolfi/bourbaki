\section*{Definition}

A \t{continuous-time time-invariant linear dynamical system} is a tuple $(A, B, C, D)$ where $A \in \R ^{n \times n}$, $B \in \R ^{n \times m}$, $C \in \R ^{k \times n}$ and $D \in \R ^{k \times m}$.
Given an \t{input} $u: \R  \to \R ^m$, it models a \t{state} $x: \R  \to \R ^n$ and \t{output} $y: \R  \to \R ^k$ by
  \[
\begin{aligned}
\dot{x} &= Ax + Bu, \\
y &= Cx + Du.
\end{aligned}
  \]
Here $Ax$ is called the \t{drift term} and $Bu$ is called the input term (of $\dot{x}$).
