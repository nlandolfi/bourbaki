\ssection{Results}

The \ct{moment generating function}{}
of a real-valued random variable is
the function mapping real numbers to
the expectation of the exponential
of the product of that real number
with the random variable.

\ssubsection{Notation}

Let $(X, \SA, \mu)$ be a
probability space.
Let $f$ be a real-valued
random variable on $X$.
Let $R$ denote the
real numbers.
For each $t \in R$,
denote by
$tf$ the function
$x \mapsto tf(x)$.
Similarly, denote by
$e^{tf}$ the funciton
$x \mapsto e^{tf(x)}$.

Denote the moment
generating function
of $f$ by
$m_f: R \to R$.
We defined it by
\[
  m_f(t) = \Expect(e^{tf}).
\]
