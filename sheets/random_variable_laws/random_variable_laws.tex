\stitle{Random Variable Laws}

\ssection{Why}

We name the image
measures of
real-valued random
variables.

\ssection{Definition}

The
\ct{law}{randomvariablelaw}
of a real-valued
random variable,
using the topological sigma algebra,
is the image measure
of the probability
measure under the
random variable
Recall that


\ssubsection{Notation}

Let
$(X, \mathcal{A})$
and
$(Y, \mathcal{B})$
be two measurable spaces.
Let $f: X \to Y$ be
a random variable.
Let
$\mu: \mathcal{A} \to \nneri$
be a probability measure.
We denote the
law of $f$
by $\rvlaw{\mu}{f}$.
