\stitle{Permutation Matrices}

\ssection{Why}

Permutations of the set $\set{1, 2, \dots, n}$ correspond to matrices over $\N$.

\ssection{Definition}

The \t{matrix} corresponding to a permutation of $\set{1, 2, \dots, n}$ is a matrix of natural numbers whose $i,j$th element is 1 if the permutation maps $i$ to $j$; for $i, j \in \set{1, 2, \dots, n}$.

\ssection{Notation}

Let $\sigma: \set{1, 2, \dots, n} \to \set{1, 2, \dots, n}$ be a permutation.
Let $M \in \Mat{\N}{n}{n}$ be such that $M_{ij}$ is one if $\sigma(i) = j$ and 0 otherwise.
Then $M$ is the permutation matrix of $\sigma$.
