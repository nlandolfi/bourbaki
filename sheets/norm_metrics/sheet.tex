
\section*{Why}

If we have a norm, then we have a metric.

\section*{Motivating result}

\begin{proposition}
Let $(V, F)$ be a vector space.
Let $f: V \to \R $ be a norm.
Let $g: V \times  V \to \R $ such that
\[
g(x, y) = \norm{x - y}.
\]
Then $g$ is a metric.\footnote{Future editions will include an account.}
\end{proposition}
