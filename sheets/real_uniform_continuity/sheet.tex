\ssubsection{Notation}

Let $R$ denote the set of real numbers.
Let $f: R \to R$. Then $f$ is uniformly continuous
at $x \in R$ if
\[
  (\forall \epsilon > 0)(\exists \delta > 0)(\forall x \in R)(\abs{x - y} < \delta \implies \abs{f(x) - f(y)} < \epsilon)
\]
for all $y \in R$.
