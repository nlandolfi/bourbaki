Matroids

Why

We generalize the notion of linear dependence.

Definition

A matroid is a \rt{finite subset algebra}{finitesubsetalgebra} satisfying: \begin{enumerate}

\item The \rt{subset}{subset} of a \rt{distinguished set}{distinguishedsubset} is \rt{distinguished}{distinguishedsubset}.

\item For two \rt{distinguished}{distinguishedsubset} \rt{subsets}{subset} of nonequal \rt{cardinality}{cardinality}, there is an \rt{element of}{element} the \rt{base set}{baseset} in the complement of the smaller set in the bigger set whose singleton union with the smaller set is a \rt{distinguished}{distinguishedsubset} set. \end{enumerate}

An independent subset of a \rt{matroid}{matroid} is a \rt{distinguished subset}{distinguishedsubset}. A depenedent subset of a \rt{matroid}{matroid} is an \rt{undistinguished subset}{undistinguishedsubset}.

Notation

We follow the notation of subset algebras, but use $M$ for the base set, a mnemonic for matroid, and $\mathcal{I}$ for the distinguished sets, a menomic for independent.

Let $(M, \mathcal{I})$ a matroid. We denote the properties by \begin{enumerate} \item $A \in \mathcal{I} \land B \subset A \implies B \in \mathcal{I}$. \item $A, B \in \mathcal{I} \land \card{A} < \card{B} \implies \exists x \in M: (A \union \set{x}) \in \mathcal{I}$ \end{enumerate}
