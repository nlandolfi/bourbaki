Measurable Sections

Why

Toward a theory of iterated integrals, we need to know that set and function sections are measurable.

Results

Proposition Let $(X, \SA)$ and $(Y, \SB)$ be measurable spaces. For any $E \in \SA \cross \SB$, the sections $E_x$ and $E^y$ are measurable for any $x \in X$ and $y \in Y$.

Proposition Let $(X, \SA)$ and $(Y, \SB)$ be measurable spaces. Let $f: X \cross Y \to F$, where $F$ is the extended real numnbers or the complex numbers, and $f$ is measurable (using the appropriate sigma algebra of the codomain). The sections $f_x: Y \to F$ and $f^y: X \to F$ are measurable for each $x \in X$ and $y \in Y$.
