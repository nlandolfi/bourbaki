Result

Proposition Let $(X, \SA, \mu)$ and $(Y, \SB, \nu)$ be $\sigma$-finite measurable spaces. Let $f: X \cross Y \to \eri$ be $\SA \cross \SB$-measurable and $\mu\cross\nu$-integrable. Then
  1. For $\mu$-almost every $x$ in $X$ the section $f_x$ is $\nu$-integrable and for $\nu$-almost every $y$ in $Y$ the section $f^y$ is $\mu$-integrable,
  2. the functions $I_f$ and $J_f$ defined by \[ I_f(x) = \begin{cases} \int_{Y} f_x d\nu &\text{ if } f_x \text{ is } \nu\text{-integrable}, \\ 0 & \text{ otherwise} \end{cases} \] and \[ J_f(y) = \begin{cases} \int_{X} f^y d\mu &\text{ if } f^y \text{ is } \mu\text{-integrable}, \\ 0 & \text{ otherwise} \end{cases} \] belong to $\SL(X, \SA, \mu, R)$ and $\SL(Y, \SB, \nu, R)$ respectively, and
    3. \item the relation \[ \int_{X \cross Y} f d(\mu \cross \nu) = \int_{X} I_f d\mu = \int_{Y} J_f d\nu \] holds.
The above is called Fubini's Theorem
