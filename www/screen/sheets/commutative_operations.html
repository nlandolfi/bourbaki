Commutative Operations

Why

We introduce language for the case in which an operation does not depend on the order in which it operates.

Definition

An operation commutes if the result of two elements is the same regardless of their order.

Notation

Let $A$ be a non-empty set and let $+: A \times A \to A$ be an operation. If $+$ commutes, then $$ a + b = b + a $$ for all $a, b \in A$.
