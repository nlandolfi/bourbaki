\stitle{Homomorphism}

\ssection{Why}

We name a function which preserves group structure.

\ssection{Definition}


A \definition{homomorphism} from group
$(A, +)$ to group $(B, \tilde{+})$
is a function $f: A \to B$ such that
$f(e_A) = f(e_B)$ for identities $e_A \in A$
and $e_B \in B$ and $f(a + a') = f(a) \tilde{+} f(a')$
for all $a, a' \in A$.
